# P16 predeclared capacity-growth rule

This rule was fixed before any P16 corpus-rate output was produced. It is the
only capacity-growth rule permitted for this experiment.

## Fixed inputs and starting plan

P16 retains P15's exact per-block frequencies, E and B proposals, one P15
global code-length reassignment, exact `SpatialCostPlan`, strict single-group
fallback, and selected-only packed writer. The growth start is the complete
RIFF-byte winner of E, B, and P15-refined, with ties ordered E, then B, then
P15-refined. No third color signature is introduced.

For an exact histogram `h`, let `C(h)` be the exact number of VP8L bits needed
for its five serialized adaptive table headers plus the payload generated by
`h` under those tables, including length and distance extra bits. For two
histograms, addition is component-wise exact addition and

`merge_penalty(a, b) = C(a + b) - C(a) - C(b)`.

This distance is support-safe: every symbol in either operand is present in
the combined histogram, so the combined tables can encode the union of both
supports. It never evaluates one singleton under another singleton's
codebook, does not smooth counts, and has no absent-symbol infinity. Length and
distance extra-bit costs are included in `C`; because they are additive they
cancel in the penalty, which is checked in tests rather than assumed by the
implementation.

## One deterministic growth iteration

An iteration is allowed only while the current group count is below the
profile's existing `maximum_groups`.

1. A block is splittable only when its current source group contains at least
   two non-empty exact block histograms. Its coding regret is its exact payload
   cost under the source group's current five code tables minus its exact
   payload cost under tables built from the block itself. Table-header cost is
   deliberately excluded from regret because regret chooses a block within an
   already-materialized group; complete headers are charged by the later RIFF
   acceptance test. Select the greatest regret, then the smallest raster-order
   block index. Its current group is the source group and the block is seed 1.
2. Among the other non-empty blocks in that source group, choose seed 2 by the
   greatest exact `merge_penalty(seed1, block)`, then the smallest raster-order
   block index.
3. Seed 1 remains in the old source group and seed 2 enters the new group. For
   every other block in the source group, compare
   `merge_penalty(seed1, block)` with `merge_penalty(seed2, block)`; choose the
   smaller penalty and retain seed 1/the old group on a tie. Blocks outside the
   source group retain their assignments. Empty spatial blocks retain their
   existing filled assignment and do not participate in regret, seeding, or
   partition distance.
4. Rebuild all exact group frequencies. Then perform exactly one global P15
   code-length reassignment: a block retains its current group on an equal
   payload cost; among strict improvements the lowest group id wins. Compact
   empty groups in ascending old-group order and rebuild once more.
5. Fully cost the candidate, including the nested group map, all five table
   headers per group, weighted payload, length/distance extra bits, byte
   rounding, VP8L padding, and complete RIFF bytes. Accept only if complete
   RIFF bytes strictly decrease relative to the previous accepted plan. A tie
   or increase retains the previous plan and terminates growth.

Growth also terminates at the profile group cap or when the post-reassignment
assignments equal the previous accepted assignments. Rates are monotone at
every accepted step by construction. Final complete-file selection compares
E, B, P15-refined, the final accepted split plan, and single. Spatial ties keep
E, then B, then P15-refined, then split; single wins every complete-RIFF tie.

## Fixed non-goals

There is no random input, corpus-tuned threshold, image-id rule, classifier,
graph-id rule, third signature, dependency, thread, unsafe code, public API,
Default-profile, metadata, animation, or error-semantics change.
